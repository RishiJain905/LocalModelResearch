## 1. Definitions and Standards
### Vision Encoding (Formal Definition)
**Source:** Awesome-Visual-Tokenizers (GitHub lavinal712)
**URL:** https://github.com/lavinal712/Awesome-Visual-Tokenizers/blob/main/README.md

> "A visual tokenizer is a mechanism that maps input visual signals (such as images or videos) into a set of compact and structured visual units (tokens), which may be continuous vectors, discrete indices, or a hybrid of both. A core requirement of a visual tokenizer is that the generated visual units must possess sufficient representational capacity to enable high-quality reconstruction of the original visual input through a corresponding decoder or generator."

### Vision Transformer (ViT) Patch Encoding
**Source:** "An Image is Worth 16x16 Words: Transformers for Image Recognition at Scale" (Dosovitskiy et al., ICLR 2021)
**arXiv:** https://arxiv.org/abs/2010.11929

> "An image is split into smaller fixed-sized patches which are treated as a sequence of tokens, similar to words for NLP tasks."

### Multimodal LLM Architecture
**Source:** MME-Survey: A Comprehensive Survey on Evaluation of Multimodal LLMs (arXiv:2411.15296)
**URL:** https://arxiv.org/html/2411.15296v1

> "A typical vision-language MLLM comprises three modules: (1) Modality encoder (e.g., vision encoder); (2) Connector (aligns vision features to text embedding space); (3) LLM (generates responses from concatenated multimodal inputs)."
