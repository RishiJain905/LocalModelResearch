## Foundational Papers

### Model Cards for Model Reporting (2019)

> *"Model Cards for Model Reporting"*
> — Margaret Mitchell, Simone Wu, Andrew Zaldivar, et al.
> — FAT* 2019 (Atlanta, GA)
> — [arXiv:1810.03993](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.03993) | DOI: 10.1145/3287560.3287596

First framework proposing structured model documentation. Model cards are short documents accompanying trained ML models detailing benchmarked evaluation across demographic/intersectional subgroups.

> *"We propose a framework that we call Model Cards, to accompany Machine Learning models, disclosing information such as the recommended use cases and limitations, factors that may impact model performance across demographic groups, and ideally optimization behavior."*

Core inspiration for Hugging Face, Google, Amazon, NVIDIA, SAS model documentation.

---

### Datasheets for Datasets (2018/2021)

> *"Datasheets for Datasets"*
> — Timnit Gebru, B. et al.
> — Communications of the ACM, December 2021 (first posted arXiv:1803.09010, 2018)
> — [arXiv:1803.09010](https://arxiv.org/abs/1803.09010)

Analogous to electronics industry component datasheets; proposes 7 documentation dimensions for datasets:
- Motivation
- Composition
- Collection Process
- Preprocessing/Cleaning/Labeling
- Maintenance
- Recommended Uses
- Distribution

> *"Analogous to datasheets for electronic components, datasheets for datasets document their provenance, composition, collection process, label annotation process, intended uses, maintenance, and other information."*

Directly referenced in EU AI Act practical guidance.

### Systematic Analysis of 32K AI Model Cards (2024)

> *"What's documented in AI? Systematic Analysis of 32K AI Model Cards"*
> — [arXiv:2402.05160v1](https://arxiv.org/abs/2402.05160v1)

**Analyzed 32,111 Hugging Face model cards. Key findings:**

| Finding | Statistic |
|---------|-----------|
| Models with cards | 44.2% |
| Mention Limitations | 17.4% |
| Have Evaluation details | 15.4% |
| Have Environmental Impact | 2.0% |
| Environmental sections auto-generated | 84.8% |
| Cards account of download traffic | 90.5% |

> *"84.8% of Environmental Impact sections are auto-generated by AutoNLP/AutoTrain — suggesting documentation quality is severely compromised by automation without human oversight."*

### Use Case Cards: EU AI Act Inspired (2024)

> *"Use case cards: A use case reporting framework inspired by the European AI Act"*
> — Hupont, Fernández-Llorca, Baldassarri, Gómez
> — Ethics and Information Technology, Springer, March 2024
> — [DOI: 10.1007/s10676-024-09757-7](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10676-024-09757-7)

UML-based methodology documenting intended purpose and operational use, aligned with EU AI Act risk-based approach. 9 structured fields including:
- Intended purpose
- Type of product
- Application area
- Stakeholders
- Foreseeable misuses

### Automated Model Card Generation: CardGen (NAACL 2024)

> *"Automatic Generation of Model and Data Cards: A Step Towards Responsible AI"*
> — Liu, Li, Jin, Diab (CMU, MPI, ETH Zürich)
> — NAACL 2024
> — [arXiv:2405.06258](https://arxiv/html/2405.06258v1) | [GitHub](https://github.com/jiarui-liu/AutomatedModelCardGeneration)

**CardGen pipeline:** Two-step retrieval-augmented generation using LLMs.
- **CardBench:** 4,829 model cards + 865 data cards with paired source documents
- GPT-4 outperforms humans on completeness but lags on accuracy due to hallucination

> *"We introduce CardGen, an automatic generation pipeline using retrieval-augmented LLM generation. We also introduce CardBench, a large benchmark for evaluating model/data card generation."*

### AI Cards: Machine-Readable EU AI Act (2024)

> *"AI Cards: Towards an Applied Framework for Machine-Readable AI and Risk Documentation"*
> — [arXiv:2406.18211](https://arxiv.org/abs/2406.18211)
> — Accepted at Annual Privacy Forum / Springer LNCS, September 2024

Proposes dual-format framework:
1. **Human-readable visual card** — structured summary for stakeholders
2. **Machine-readable Semantic Web spec** — W3C standards (DCAT-AP, RDF/OWL)

Nine structured information elements aligned with EU AI Act Art. 11, Annex IV, Art. 9.

> *"AI Cards are designed for value-chain interoperability: providers, deployers, auditors, and conformity assessment bodies can all consume the same machine-readable artifact."*

### AI Model Passport (2025)

> *"AI Model Passport: Data and system traceability framework for transparent AI in health"*
> — Computational and Structural Biotechnology Journal, Vol. 28, 2025, pp. 386-404
> — [DOI: 10.1016/j.csbj.2025.09.041](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2001037025004015) (Open Access)

Digital identity and verification tool for AI models in healthcare. Captures metadata for:
- Unique identification
- Traceability

## Regulatory Frameworks

### EU AI Act (Regulation 2024/1689)

**Full Text:** [artificialintelligenceact.eu](https://artificialintelligenceact.eu/)

#### Key Documentation Requirements

**Article 11 & Annex IV — High-Risk AI Systems:**
> *"Technical documentation shall be drawn up before the placing on the market or putting into service of a high-risk AI system and shall be kept up to date."*

Required content (Annex IV):
- Intended purpose, system architecture, data governance (Art. 10)
- Risk management (Art. 9), monitoring, accuracy/limitations
- Human oversight (Art. 14), technical robustness, cybersecurity

**Article 53 & Annex XI/XII — General Purpose AI (GPAI):**
- Describing architecture, parameters, modality
- Versions, acceptable use policies
- Summary of training data, methodology

**Chapter III — High-Risk Systems (selected):**
- Biometrics (Art. 5)
- Critical infrastructure (Art. 6)
- Education and vocational training (Art. 7)
- Employment and workforce management (Art. 8)

### NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF 1.0)

**Published:** January 26, 2023 | [PDF](https://nvlpubs.nist.gov/nistpubs/ai/nist.ai.100-1.pdf)

**Four core functions:**

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Govern** | Foster risk-aware culture of accountability, transparency, trustworthiness |
| **Map** | Contextualize AI system risks relative to intended use |
| **Measure** | Analyze and assess AI risks; support tracking |
| **Manage** | Prioritize and act on AI risks; monitor continuously |

> *"The AI Risk Management Framework is intended to be a living document that evolves with technological advancements, lessons learned from implementation, and community feedback."*

**AI RMF Playbook:** [rai-toolkit.github.io](https://rai-toolkit.github.io/) | [Direct NIST link](https://www.nist.gov/itl/ai-risk-management-framework/nist-ai-rmf-playbook)

### ISO/IEC Standards

#### ISO/IEC 42001:2023 — AI Management System

Specifies requirements for establishing, implementing, maintaining, improving AI governance. First international AI management system standard.

> *"This document is intended to help organizations including companies, governmental agencies, and non-governmental organizations to practice responsible AI development."*

Microsoft 365 Copilot certified under ISO 42001. — [Microsoft certificate](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/compliance/regulatory/offering-iso-42001)

#### ISO/IEC 23894:2023 — AI Risk Management Guidance

Complements ISO 42001; provides guidance but not certifiable requirements.

#### Alignment Matrix

| Framework | Key Focus |
|-----------|-----------|
| EU AI Act | Regulatory compliance, market placement |
| NIST AI RMF | Voluntary risk management |
| ISO 42001 | Certifiable management system |
| ISO 23894 | Risk management guidance |

## Key Findings

1. **Model Cards are the dominant standard** — Originally Google's 2019 FAT* paper, now adopted by Hugging Face (40k+ model cards), Microsoft, Amazon, NVIDIA, SAS. However, completeness is poor: only ~17% mention limitations, ~15% have evaluation details.

2. **Automated generation is active research** — CardGen (CMU/ETH 2024) shows LLMs can outperform humans on completeness but lag on accuracy due to hallucination. CardBench (4,829 model cards) is the largest curated dataset.

3. **EU AI Act is the regulatory driver** — Annex IV for high-risk systems is the de facto documentation template. No machine-readable format mandated, creating a gap that academic frameworks (AI Cards, practical-ai-act) are trying to fill.

4. **AI Model Passport** (2025, health/ProCAncer-I) is the most passport-like concept — provides unique verifiable identity, full lifecycle traceability, automated metadata collection.

5. **ISO 42001** (2023) provides certifiable AI management system framework, aligned with both EU AI Act and NIST AI RMF.

6. **MLflow Model Registry** is the most widely deployed MLOps platform being adapted for compliance — active GitHub discussion about adding first-class EU AI Act fields.
